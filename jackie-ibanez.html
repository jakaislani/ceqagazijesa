Jackie Ibanez- Professional Career

After graduating, Jackie went to work for the local television station WWLP-TV in Massachusetts. Despite barely being in the industry for four months, she was hired as a producer and quickly moved to the positions of reporter and anchor.

She was the managing director of Coltrin & Associates, a public relations agency, until 2010 when she left the station and journalism to become the managing director of WWLP-TV. Despite this, she returned to journalism in 2011 as a freelance anchor/reporter for News 12, Connecticut, and was elevated to weekend morning anchor the following year, a position she held until December 2014, when she departed the network.

Jackie’s decision to leave News 12 Connecticut was a sensible one because she received an offer from the Fox News Network and joined the prominent organization as a news journalist. Jackie’s name has been well-known since joining the network, as she has contributed her skills and experience to various high-profile Fox News programs, including “Fox and Friends” (2015-2018), “Red Eye w/Tom Shillue” (2017), and “Fox and Friends First” (2017-2018). This has elevated her to celebrity status and significantly boosted her riches.
