Jackie Jackson: A Look into the Life of This Multitalented Singer

Jackie Jackson’s Bio: Growing Up in the Jackson Family

Born on May 4, 1951, in Gary, Indiana, Sigmund Esco “Jackie” Jackson is a veteran singer, songwriter, and musician. Jackson grew up in a large family with nine siblings, including the late Michael Jackson.

In the mid-1960s, Jackson and his brothers Tito, Jermaine, Marlon, and Michael formed the iconic musical quintet, The Jackson 5, which thrilled music lovers worldwide. Jackson’s passion for music and desire to entertain took him on a journey of celebrity, which continues to this day.

The Current Age of Jackie Jackson and His Family life

As of 2021, Jackie Jackson is 70 years old (born in 1951), but he’s still active in the music industry with several projects and collaborations. Despite his busy musical career, Jackson maintains a private family life and endeavors to spend as much time with his loved ones as possible.

Jackie Jackson married Enid Arden Span in 1974, and while their marriage didn’t last long due to irreconcilable differences, they had two kids together: a daughter, Sigmund Esco Jackson Jr., and a son, Brandi Jackson, both of whom are now grown up and have families of their own.

Jackson’s Height and Appearance: How He Looks

Jackie Jackson is the third tallest in The Jackson 5 at 6 feet, only being shorter than his brothers Marlon and Jermaine. With a great sense of style, Jackie appears polished and suave both on and off stage. He loves to enchant audiences with his remarkable voice, a unique blend of soul and pop with an excellent stage presence.

Jackie Jackson’s Career: Songs, Collaborations, Tours, and More

After a successful stint with The Jackson 5, Jackson went solo with his musical career. He recorded two solo albums, “Be the One” in 1989 and “Jackie Jackson” in 1973. While they didn’t get as much recognition as Jackson hoped, his creative talent was evident in his captivating music.

Jackie Jackson has also worked on several worthy collaborations throughout his career. In 1984, he worked with fellow musicians La Toya Jackson, Freddie Mercury, and Michael Jackson on the song “State of Shock.” In 1995, he joined his brothers for “The Jacksons-Unity Tour.” The trio got international recognition and sold millions of albums, including “Victory,” released in 1984.

The Top Songs of Jackie Jackson: Listen to His Best Hits

Jackie Jackson has been in the music industry for over five decades, and his musical prowess is evident in his list of successful songs. Some of his most outstanding hits include:

  • Don’t you want Me
  • Cruzin’
  • We know what’s going on
  • The Hurt
  • Be The One
  • Stay
  • Is It Him or Me?
  • Stay

Jackie Jackson’s Net Worth: How Much is He Worth?

Though Jackie Jackson’s net worth is not disclosed in public, he is estimated to be worth around $200 million, thanks to his successful music contributions and investments. Alongside his music career, he’s involved in several business ventures, including real estate.

Jackie Jackson’s legacy: What Others Say

Jackie Jackson remains a respected figure in the music industry and is loved for his contributions to shaping modern-day music. Here’s what a few well-known musicians and family members say about him:

  • “Jackie is one of the most talented and supportive people I know. He has an original style of music that has stood the test of time,” said Stevie Wonder.
  • “Jackie is an underrated genius musician who has had an impressive impact on the culture of music. It’s hip, memorable, often surprising or daring without ever losing its accessibility,” said Logan Shaw, the music critic.
  • “Watching Jackie write music is always amazing. He’ll write a song in ten minutes and make a hit out of it,” said his brother Tito Jackson.

Final Word

Despite starting his music career at an early age, Jackie Jackson has maintained his relevance in the industry to this day. His contributions to shaping modern music are invaluable, and his music will continue to inspire and entertain music lovers worldwide for generations to come.
