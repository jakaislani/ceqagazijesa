Is Walter White A Narcissist? Breaking Bad Character Explained

Posted by Martina Birk on Wednesday, July 31, 2024

Portrayed by Bryan Cranston, Walter Hartwell White Sr., the protagonist of the American Series Breaking Bad, also goes by the name ‘Heisenberg,’ his alter ego.

Is Walter White A Narcissist, Ego-Maniac, or – a Sociopath? The personality of this Breaking Bad Character has remained as intriguing as it was a decade ago.

The character is considered an antihero, but viewers tend to lose sympathy towards him when he starts hurting Jesse, from killing his girlfriend to poisoning Brock.

Is Walter White A Narcissist? Breaking Bad Character Personality Explained

A character’s personality is subjective; what one person may perceive as a positive attribute, another may view as a dangerous flaw.

Some think that Walter White had an egomaniacal personality, as he frequently put himself in danger to help Jesse, yet he also betrayed him.

Walt’s inability to feel happy for Junior receiving recognition and money from the website was because he viewed it as charity and believed it was his own money and glory for which his son was taking credit.

Additionally, he put Hank back on the Heisenberg trail because he resented Gale receiving credit for his work.

Although his personality resembles sociopathy and ego mania, I believe Walter White is narcissistic.

Sociopaths tend to view themselves as superior beings but are aware of their limitations, whereas narcissists lack this self-awareness.

Walt believes in his perfection and desires everyone around him to acknowledge and recognize this belief.

The protagonist demonstrates significant emotional distress in response to any challenge to his perceived persona as a virtuous individual or a criminal mastermind.

He tends to shift the blame onto others, particularly Jesse, or attribute his failures to external factors beyond his control.

Walt persisted in the meth business not solely for monetary gain or authority but to boost his self-importance.

His need to be the ultimate Leader in the industry became a source of personal pride that he couldn’t relinquish.

So for the question, Is Walter White A Narcissist? Yes, the Breaking Bad Protagonist certainly is! Well, at least, in my opinion.

Crazy Theory That Makes Sense: Walter White Mimics His Kills

In the early episodes of Breaking Bad, Walt is characterized as a mild-mannered and cautious high school teacher reluctant to turn to violence.

As Walt’s criminal endeavors expand and he starts getting recognized as Heisenberg, he becomes increasingly willing to take human lives.

He started as a method to shield Walter’s real identity from unsavory individuals such as Tuco but quickly became a point of pride for him.

Heisenberg’s drive to be the best has resulted in the downfall of past drug kingpins like Krazy-8, Tuco, Hector Salamanca, and Gustavo Fring, making it essential for others to recognize his “mad determination.”

Fans of Breaking Bad have noted an alarming tendency in how he carries out these killings.

Per this theory, killing someone has a transformative effect on Walt, causing him to take on certain traits or tendencies of his victims.

Following the murder of Crazy-8, Walt is seen to adopt the same practice of cutting the crusts off his sandwiches, which some interpret as an indication of how the dead man has left his mark on Walt.

As the series progresses, Walt starts driving a vehicle resembling Gus’ car and begins consuming the same drink Mike favors.
