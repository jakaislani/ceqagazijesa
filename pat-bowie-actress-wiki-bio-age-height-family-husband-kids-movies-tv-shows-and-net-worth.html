Pat Bowie Biography

Pat Bowie is an actress born and raised in the United States. She has graced stages across the United States and London playing notable roles such as Aunt Ester in August Wilson’s Gem of the Ocean, Mrs. Zulu in Tug Yourgrau’s Song of Jacob Zulu, and Amanda in Noel Coward’s Private Lives.

She is known for her work in Waking the Dead (2000), Ben Elton: The Man from Auntie (1990) and Meet Cute (2022). Her most recent role was in the TV series And Just Like That where she plays Eunice, Herbert’s mother.

Bowie was awarded a 1979 Joseph Jefferson Citation for Actress in a Principal Role in a Play for “Boseman and Lena” at the Chicago Theatre Company in Chicago, Illinois.

Career

Bowie is a gifted actress who has appeared in theatres in the US and in London in roles including Amanda in Noel Coward’s Private Lives, Mrs. Zulu in Tug Yourgrau’s Song of Jacob Zulu, and Aunt Ester in August Wilson’s Gem of the Ocean.

She gained notoriety for her performances in the films Meet Cute (2022), Ben Elton: The Man from Auntie (1990), and Waking the Dead (2000). She received a Joseph Jefferson Citation in 1979 for her performance in “Boseman and Lena” at the Chicago Theatre Company in Chicago, Illinois, for an actress in a principal role in a play.

She received a 1986 Joseph Jefferson Award nomination for Actress in a Supporting Role in a Play for her performance in “American Dreams” at the Victory Gardens Theater in Chicago, Illinois.
